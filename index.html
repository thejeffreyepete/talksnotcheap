A word before you decide

Dear Friend,

Let me ask you something honest.

You can speak. Maybe you speak regularly — at events, in meetings, on social media, in front of clients. People listen. Some even applaud.

Is the money coming?

And I don't only mean the fee on a cheque. I mean — are your words landing? Are they changing anything? Are people walking away from your presentations different from how they came in? Because the gap between speaking and truly communicating is where most people silently live — and most never find their way out.

I know this gap because I lived in it.

I started public speaking at twelve years old — church convention, sweaty palms, a notebook full of points I finished reading in five minutes when I had fifteen. The applause I got that day was the kind that says "you'll do better, boy."

Several years later, the same voice that embarrassed me in that church hall has taken me to stages across Nigeria — and people now take notes even during casual conversations. That last sentence is not a flex. It is what this book can do for you.

The difference between who I was and who I became is not talent. It is not confidence. It is not even "just practice".

It is a set of principles — about identity, about structure, about how a voice becomes a force — that I have now put into one book.

That book is called Talk's Not Cheap. And I believe it will change how you see yourself every time you open your mouth — and what happens in every room because of it.

Read on.
